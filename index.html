            The Con Artist

            Traits

            Your strengths, weaknesses, skills, habits, and quirks.

              Feats

              Special actions that only you can perform.

              • Sense Disposition: You are an uncannily accurate judge of character, and can tell at a glance if someone is particularly gullible, vulnerable, upset, or attracted to someone.
              • Charm: In social situations, if you can share some fact or story about yourself that your speaking partner has in common or finds relatable, they'll be more likely to share information or do you small favors. The more details you know about them, the easier it is for you to find (or make you're no stranger to lying) something in common.
              • Seduce: If someone seems attracted to you, you can distract them with some light flirting. If you hit it off, you can even ask to head back to their place (or maybe somewhere nearby), which is an ideal opportunity to catch them in a vulnerable position.
              • Pickpocket: If someone's distracted or inattentive it's easy for you to relieve them of their wallet, keys, phone, or any other loose item. These can be useful on their own, but returning an item they "dropped" can also be a great way of getting on their good side.

                  The Muscle

                  Traits

                  Your strengths, weaknesses, skills, habits, and quirks.

                    Feats

                    Special actions that only you can perform.

                    • Escalate: If you find yourself in a tense situation, you can either taunt the opposing party into making the first move OR pacify them with threats of extreme retaliation. It's your choice... which may end up meaning more violence than not.
                    • Kidnap: It's not your favorite activity, but you can stuff someone into a car if need be. The best results come from choosing a location with little security, driving up next to the target, and requesting they get into the car. Works best with two people and a gun.
                    • Interrogate: If you're in a secured location with someone who's vulnerable, you can interrogate them. In your experience, almost everyone tells you what you want to know without you having to hurt them. Slowly revealing a variety of devilish tools is enough.
                    • Threaten: Most people will call the authorities if you let them go after kidnapping / interrogating / harming them... that is, unless you give them reason not to. A thinly-veiled reminder of the consequences is enough to keep most from ratting you out.

                              Traits

                              Your strengths, weaknesses, skills, habits, and quirks.

                                • Lockpick: A lifetime of breaking and entering has left you with the skills to pick any lock that isn't keycard-operated or a vault. You know from personal experience that best results come from having a second person as a lookout, otherwise you arouse suspicion.
